# Naming rules

## Product / brand
- **ZenSolar** — one word, capital Z, capital S. Never "Zen Solar", "Zensolar", or "zensolar".
- Used everywhere user-facing: marketing, app UI, investor demo, white paper, social.

## Legal entity
- **ZenCorp Inc** — the corporation that holds IP, contracts, and equity.
- Joke/intent: plays on the "faceless megacorp" trope while the actual product is a clean-energy network. Use sparingly and with a knowing wink — never as the primary brand.
- Appropriate contexts: footer fine print ("© 2026 ZenCorp Inc."), terms/privacy, cap table, employment docs, investor legal docs, SAFE/SAFT paperwork.
- **Do not** use "ZenCorp" in product UI, marketing headlines, or anywhere a user would mistake it for the brand.

## Why
The product earns trust by being warm, human, and clean (ZenSolar). The corporation can be the dry legal vehicle (ZenCorp Inc). Keeping them visually distinct protects the brand while giving the founders the inside joke.
