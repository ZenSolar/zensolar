# Naming rules

## Product / brand
- **ZenSolar** — one word, capital Z, capital S. Never "Zen Solar", "Zensolar", or "zensolar".
- Used everywhere user-facing: marketing, app UI, investor demo, white paper, social.

## Legal entity
- **ZenCorp Inc** — the corporation that holds IP, contracts, and equity.
- Appropriate contexts: footer fine print ("© 2026 ZenCorp Inc."), terms/privacy, cap table, employment docs, investor legal docs, SAFE/SAFT paperwork.
- **Do not** use "ZenCorp" in product UI, marketing headlines, or anywhere a user would mistake it for the brand.

## The joke (preserve it — it's strategic, not just cute)
"ZenCorp Inc" is a deliberate sarcastic wink. Only an incorporated entity (C-corp, etc.) can IPO on a public exchange — and ZenSolar will **never IPO**. The whole thesis is that the $ZSOLAR token *is* the liquidity event, replacing the traditional IPO path entirely. So we stuck the most corporate-sounding name possible ("Corp Inc") onto the most anti-corporate exit strategy possible.

The joke works on three levels:
1. **Surface:** It sounds like a generic megacorp (Initech, Umbrella, OsCorp).
2. **Industry insider:** Crypto-natives recognize that "Inc" = the thing you'd need to IPO, which we're explicitly rejecting.
3. **Founders' wink:** Joseph and Michael named the legal shell "Corp" precisely *because* the product will never behave like one.

Keep this context — if a future writer/designer sees only "ZenCorp Inc" in the footer, they should know it's intentional irony, not a branding mistake to "fix."

## Why
The product earns trust by being warm, human, and clean (ZenSolar). The corporation is the dry legal vehicle (ZenCorp Inc). Keeping them visually distinct protects the brand while preserving the founders' inside joke about never IPO-ing.
